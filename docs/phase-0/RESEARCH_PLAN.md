# Research Plan

Phase 0 research tests behaviour, not politeness.

## Participants

| Segment | Target |
|---|---:|
| Serious/repeat UGC NET aspirants | 8–12 |
| First-time aspirants | 3–5 |
| Tutors/coaching operators | 2–3 |
| Prototype usability participants | 5+ |

## Interview rules

- Ask about the last real study session, not an imagined future.
- Capture tools, screenshots, purchases, cancellations, scores and workflows.
- Do not pitch before understanding current behaviour.
- Separate quotations from researcher interpretation.
- Obtain consent before recording personally identifiable information.

## Core interview prompts

### Behaviour

- Walk me through your last practice session.
- How did you decide what to study?
- What happened after your last wrong answer?
- Where do you obtain questions and answer keys?
- How do you decide that a source is trustworthy?

### Failure

- Which errors keep returning?
- When did a mock result last change your study plan?
- What makes you abandon a test-prep app?
- Where do you waste the most preparation time?

### Trust

- Have you found a wrong answer or explanation in an app?
- What proof would make an AI explanation acceptable?
- Does year/shift/source visibility change trust?

### Payment

- What have you already paid for?
- What produced enough value to justify payment?
- Which subscription did you cancel and why?

## Prototype

Build only these screens:

1. Landing/value proposition
2. Exam confirmation
3. Question player
4. Immediate correction
5. Drill result with evidence confidence
6. Recommended next drill

### Usability tasks

Ask participants to start, answer, review a mistake, interpret the result, choose the next drill and save progress. Do not coach them.

### Pass conditions

- Median time to first answer under 60 seconds.
- At least 4/5 participants correctly explain why the next drill was recommended.
- At least 4/5 distinguish the result from a simple score screen.
- No participant believes one wrong answer proves a stable weakness.

## Targeted-vs-random test

For each eligible participant, prepare one random mixed set and one manually targeted set based on recent errors. Hide labels and ask which is more relevant and why.

Advance signal: at least 70% prefer the targeted set for specific, defensible reasons.

## Research record template

For every session record:

- participant segment and anonymised ID;
- date and moderator;
- current preparation workflow;
- repeated pain;
- trust objection;
- paid behaviour;
- prototype confusion;
- strongest evidence for/against the hypothesis;
- follow-up action.
