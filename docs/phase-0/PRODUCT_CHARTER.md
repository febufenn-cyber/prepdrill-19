# Product Charter

## Strategic position

Prepdrill is not another broad test-prep library. It is a decision system for what a learner should practise next.

## First user

**Serious UGC NET Paper 1 repeat aspirant** who:

- has attempted the examination, completed mocks, or studied most of the syllabus;
- practises through PDFs, Telegram, YouTube, books or test apps;
- experiences recurring errors but lacks a reliable revision priority;
- can sustain 5–20 minute practice sessions;
- values correctness and provenance more than unlimited content.

Beginners are research participants but not the primary product optimisation target. Coaching institutes are a later distribution/business path, not the opening product.

## Job to be done

> When I practise and make mistakes, help me understand which errors reveal real weaknesses and give me the smallest useful next drill so I improve instead of revising randomly.

## Product promise

**Study what you are weak at—not what you already know.**

Every completed drill must leave the learner with:

1. a clear result;
2. at least one understandable error correction;
3. an honest strength/weakness signal with evidence confidence;
4. a recommended next action and reason.

## Initial scope

- UGC NET Paper 1 only
- Guest-access five-question diagnostic
- Topic drills
- Canonical answer review
- Transparent weak-topic recommendation
- Progress saving after value is demonstrated

English Paper 2 enters only after Paper 1 content and relevance gates pass.

## Core loop

`answer → diagnose → explain → recommend → re-test`

The timed player is a measurement instrument. The explanation is an intervention. The weakness view is a representation. The product is the next-practice decision.

## Non-goals for Phase 0–1

- all government examinations;
- live classes or a video-course platform;
- AI-generated questions in the published bank;
- voice tutor or open-ended AI chat;
- leaderboards, social feed or badge economy;
- billing, WhatsApp automation or institute dashboards;
- claims of precise mastery from sparse data.

## Product principles

1. Trust before volume.
2. First answer before registration.
3. Evidence before personalisation claims.
4. Recommendations must be explainable.
5. A broken AI provider must not block practice.
6. Learner improvement outranks time-in-app.
7. Narrow dominance before exam expansion.
