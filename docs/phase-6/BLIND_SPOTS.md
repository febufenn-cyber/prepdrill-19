# Phase 6 Blind Spots

- Accuracy alone confuses lucky guesses with stable knowledge.
- Response time is noisy and must be bounded rather than trusted literally.
- Hint use, answer changes, confidence, and item evidence strength change how much one attempt should count.
- A weak concept can dominate every plan unless exposure and diversity limits exist.
- Recent mistakes and due re-checks are distinct from long-term weakness.
- Generated experimental items can poison mastery if not excluded by default.
- A model score without its version and rationale cannot be audited.
- Shadow experiments must never mutate production learner state.
- Calibration on synthetic trajectories does not replace real learner-outcome validation.
