# Phase 11 Build Contract — Growth, SEO, Telegram, and Attribution

## Goal

Create measurable acquisition loops from rights-cleared public educational value into personalized practice without indexing blocked content, losing attribution during guest merge, rewarding abuse, or running unbounded experiments.

## Required software

- public-page eligibility requiring active published state, clear issues, resolved rights, verified answers, and an explicit indexable flag;
- canonical metadata, robots directives, sitemap entries, and stable content fingerprints;
- signed Telegram/creator deep links with campaign and destination allow-lists;
- first-touch and last-touch attribution that survives guest-to-account merge without duplicating conversions;
- referral issuance, redemption, self-referral and device/account abuse controls;
- experiment definitions with predeclared hypothesis, primary metric, guardrails, allocation, stop conditions, and kill switch;
- conversion events tied to activation, retained learning, and paid conversion rather than clicks alone;
- channel-specific kill switches and immutable decisions;
- Phase 11 evaluator and all inherited workflows.

## Non-goals

- indexing questions with unresolved rights or publication issues;
- storing raw personal referral fingerprints;
- allowing marketing parameters to alter learner scoring or content selection truth;
- launching real campaigns without owner activation and reviewed public copy;
- claiming channel economics without real acquisition data.

## Acceptance tests

Eligible content produces indexable canonical metadata and sitemap entries; blocked, retired, experimental, or unresolved-rights content is noindex; signed deep links reject tampering and unsupported destinations; attribution survives guest merge exactly once; self-referral, repeated-device, and redemption-limit abuse fail; experiments cannot start without guardrails or while killed; conversion metrics distinguish clicks from meaningful activation; and all inherited suites remain green.
